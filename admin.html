Data sets

Your business right now

The whole operation at a glance: money, demand, and the handful of things that actually need a decision from you today. Every block is computed live from the same database the detail tabs read.

Leads

Every contact a visitor volunteered, in one stream. Each row is written to the leads table in our Supabase database the moment a form is submitted on the site: the welcome voucher popup (type Popup), the newsletter form on the magazine page (Newsletter), a booking request (Booking), a career application (Career), plus captures from the chatbot and the apartment finder quiz. The same submission also triggers the automated branded email, so what you see here is exactly what the follow-up system saw.

Accounts

Real registered users. When someone signs up on the main site (or saves a booking or voucher to a free account), Supabase creates the login and a database trigger adds a row to the profiles table, which is what this list reads. Role marks who is an admin; everyone else can only ever see their own data, enforced by row-level security in the database itself.

Bookings

One row per completed booking flow, written to the bookings table when the guest confirms the final step. Nights and total are computed live by the booking calculator (including weekly and monthly discounts and any voucher); guest contact comes from the details step. The Preference column merges what we know about how to host them: the room category and stay preference saved in their account settings, plus whatever they typed in the "make your stay perfect" box after this booking. These same rows feed the public availability calendar, which is why booked dates grey out for the next visitor.

Vouchers

Discount codes owned by accounts, from the vouchers table. The Welcome 15 voucher is issued automatically by a database trigger the moment an account is created (including signups from the welcome popup), and its status flips to used when it is applied at checkout.

Applications

Submissions from the Career page form, stored in the applications table: role applied for, name, contact and experience, exactly as the applicant typed them.

Page views

Anonymous traffic. Every page load logs a pageview event (path plus referrer, no personal data) to the events table via a small tracker script that runs on every page. The Visitors number on the overview is unique sessions counted from these events.

Password quiz

Answers from the playful forgot-password quiz, stored in the password_quiz table. While resetting a password the guest also rates the site and suggests an improvement, which turned a boring reset into a feedback channel.

Website feedback

What visitors said when they clicked "Rate my website" in the footer, plus any emails left by people interested in what we build.

Chatbot

What visitors asked the on-site chat, and the leads it captured. The chat logs every open, question, answer and miss as events in the events table; when it cannot answer, it offers a human follow-up and that contact lands in Leads with the exact question attached.

What guests ask about

Every question typed into the chat, answered or not. The bot is not here to replace customer service; it is a listening device. What people ask is what they care about, even when they never fill in a form, so the recurring questions below are free market research: pricing worries, missing information, demand signals.

Top unanswered questions

Each recurring miss is a question to add to the library, or a keyword to add to an existing answer. This is the backlog that makes the bot smarter.

Recent chatbot leads

Captures from the chat, each with the question that prompted them.

Finder quiz

The apartment finder funnel and which category guests are matched to. Finder leads (type finder_quiz) appear in the Leads tab.

Booking funnel

Where visitors drop off between landing on the booking page and confirming. This is your "why don't they book" view. Each step of the booking flow logs a funnel event to the events table as the guest moves through it, and the exit survey answers shown below come from the same table (type exit_survey), recorded when someone tells us why they are leaving.
